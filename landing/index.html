§ III What we create

A short, private video of someone you've lost.

Between 30 seconds and 3 minutes. Made from the photographs, home videos, and voice recordings you share — guided by the memories and moments you describe.

  1. i.

    A gentle portrait

    Them smiling, looking at the camera, breathing softly. Stillness, made to move.

  2. ii.

    A moment re-imagined

    Walking in their garden. Sitting in their favourite chair. Places you remember them in.

  3. iii.

    A message in their voice

    Drawn from recordings you already have. Only words they said, or you feel they would have.

  4. iv.

    A multi-scene remembrance

    Built around a story only you know. For anniversaries, weddings, children yet to come.

Every video is made for one family. It is never shared, never shown, never used for anything else.

§ IX Questions we're often asked
i. Is this disrespectful to the person who passed?

We don't think so, and we work hard to make sure it isn't. Humans have made memorial portraits, funeral photographs, and tribute films for centuries. This is in that tradition — just with a gentler, more modern tool.

ii. What do you need from me?

At minimum, a few clear photographs. More helpful: videos, voice recordings, a written description of who they were. The more we have, the more the final piece will feel like them.

iii. How long does it take?

Usually 1–2 weeks from the start of production. We don't rush.

iv. Can you create their voice?

Only if you have existing voice recordings to work from, and only saying things they actually said — or that you feel they would have. We will not invent speech.

v. What about my privacy?

Your photos, videos, and story are never shared, resold, or used to train any model. Source files are deleted after delivery at your request. Everything is stored on encrypted, private servers.

vi. What if I don't like it?

You see a preview before final delivery. We'll revise it until it feels right — or refund you if it doesn't, no questions asked.

vii. Is this appropriate for children?

That's a decision only you can make. We're happy to talk about it on the call.
